As the clock approached half past one on July 20th, an excavator  did what it never expected to do: hit an electricity cable. On that instant, hundreds of houses were haunted with the lack of electricity.

<!--more-->

Minutes passed, still nothing. Messages were flooding on group chats from different buildings. Communities coming together to understand what had just happened. Fear no more. The grid operator was already on it.

Not much later than an hour and a half past the ominous accident, light was born and electricity came rushing back to hundreds of houses. The masses had calmed. The lifts were lifting. The pumps were pumping water. Except at my place. Something else was awaiting me.

While everyone else enjoyed the graciousness of electricity, I looked mesmerized at the electric panel. Something was off. Not in the figurative way of it, the literal one. One of the main switches was off. And it did not want to get up!

After turning off every group individually, lo and behold: group 5 was causing the main switch to trip. Group 5: the greatest of them all, encompassing kitchen, living room, and office - the thing they officially call "second bedroom".

I went on a scavenging hunt and unplugged everything I could. Everything. Group 5 still caused the main switch to trip. Calls rental company. Rental company calls reparation company - it wasn't this easy however, but details will be spared. They both assure someone will show up within two hours.

No one came. The two hours passed once the hour pointer passed the six hours in the afternoon. I called again. Surprise. It's now after working hours, so one must call the emergency number of the repairs company.

Repairs company indicates that they cannot see the assignments that were made during work hours. How dare them? Called the rental company emergency number. They said they'd still come that same day.

One waits. One waits while food perishes in the fridge and freezer that only get warmer and warmer. A friend comes and takes some things to put in their own fridge and freezer, avoiding further decay.

Kept waiting and waiting. It was now late. One has stopped expecting the technician to come that day. Went to sleep, not the best. Wakes up at the peak of the six hours in the morning, after a troubled night of sleep.

Anxiously waits for it to be eight hours in the morning. Calls repairs company. Waits for 30 minutes in line. No one. Calls rental company. Immediately picked up. Rental company calls repairs company again. Repairs company calls to inform that someone should call soon to plan an appointment.

One waits. Two long hours have passed. No one calls. One calls the repairs company again. Ten minutes after one gets a call from a different company: a company that works with the company that works for the company. Complicated. They indicate the technician will be here in the early afternoon.

The technician arrives. After a long stride of testing the cables around the light fixture in the kitchen, the technician finds where the issue lies, but lo and behold: it just works again. The mystery stays a mystery but I have electricity again.

The standing theory is that was a bit of humidity in the cables and that during the checks it just dried enough. We don't know. But a day after, I now have electricity again! It's still a great mystery, and the problem might come back again, but let's hope not!
