Woah! It's that time of the month again. June has gone by very fast, full of activity, travel, and a - way too hot for The Netherlands - heatwave. Let's look back and reflect on everything that has happened this past month!

<!--more-->

## ✍️ Website {#website}

I've made a few updates to the AT Protocol integration of this website. Because I haven't posted a lot recently, I hadn't noticed that there was a very annoying bug that I introduced the [month prior](/2026/06/02/recently/). That bug was preventing the syndications to correctly be saved on the posts.

Other than that, I also updated the syndication of the photos to Bluesky to use the new gallery format, which supports up to 10 photos, instead of just the 4 photos of a default embedding. Can't wait to use it, but haven't had a reason yet!

## 🍄 Life {#life}

Quite a few things have happened, but I want to focus on Portugal! Chris and I went a week to Portugal, passing through Lisbon, my hometown, and then Faro. We met with some friends, Chris met a lot of people from my family that he hadn't met yet, and we also went to the wedding - for which I had to [drive](/2026/06/26/stepping-into-the-car/)! - of a friend of mine. It was so pretty!

## 💪 Health {#health}

Things are generally going well. I thing my wrist is a bit better? At least I've had less complaints. I still can't put my whole body's weight on it, but seems generally fine. Swimming lessons are also going great, and I'm almost getting my second diploma.

This month I also went swimming for the first time in a 50 meter swimming pool. What a length! I think it's easy to underestimate how long 50 meters is, especially for an unexperienced swimmer. Went there again today and did 800 meters. Bit tiring, but nice!

## 🍿 Entertainment {#entertainment}

This month was calmer when it comes to entertainment, but we still managed to see a [live show](/logs/performances/)! We went to *Soldaat van Oranje*, which is a Dutch play based on an autobiographic book of a resistance hero during WW II. The play has been running since 2010, and 2026 is the last year.

When it comes to [movies](/logs/movies/), we watched *Mother Mary*, *Hoppers*, and *Disclosure Day*. I absolutely loved the last one, but as with every film I love, the rating on IMDb is not that high. I would be interested in seeing a sequel.

Finally, we watched quite a few [series](/logs/shows/), or at least finished quite a few seasons: *The Testaments*, *For All Mankind*, *Amsterdam Centraal 24/7*, *Your Friends & Neighbors* and *Widow's Bay*. If I had to choose just one, I probably couldn't. I really enjoyed all of them, in different ways. For the train fans, I'd definitely recommend the documentary series about Amsterdam Centraal. It's very enjoyable!
