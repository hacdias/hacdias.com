At the end of April, I [wrote](/2026/04/27/stepping-into-the-car/) about stepping back into the driver's seat. I ended that post with two remarks: that I was going to take a refreshers lesson, and that I wanted to try a manual transmission before the lesson. It's been a while!

<!--more-->

It turns out that the idea of a refreshers lesson made me anxious enough to decide trying a manual transmission car *before* the lesson. That is how we ended up going to the Lommelse Sahara at the beginning of May and taking some [nice photos](/2026/05/03/lommelse-land/) around there.

The trip there went fine, it wasn't as smooth as with the automatic car, but I was expecting that. On the way back, I ended up stalling at the entrance of a roundabout because I forgot to downshift. But I solved that fast. In general, it was fine.

A week after, I had the lesson. Oh, the lesson. I felt so bad after it. I think I felt like the instructor was *too* negative, especially at the beginning. The car was just very new for me and there were a lot of things I had never seen before: button e-brake, brake hold, etc. Those modernities that older cars don't have.

At the end, he ended up telling me I should just drive more often to get the hang of it. During the lesson, he also gave me a few tips for things to pay more attention to, and I think I've kept them in mind since then.

Anyways, for the next month I didn't drive. I was either on vacation or just not have a reason for driving. In the meanwhile, I've been to Portugal for a friend's wedding and I ended up driving my mother's 20 year old car.

Also stalled it twice but it was quickly solved. But that car has an unusually high bite point apparently. Everyone online complaints about the clutch of this car, so I'm happy to not be the only one. The line between riding and stalling is very thin. Very annoying.

Anyways. That day I drove to the wedding and then back, at night. After fiddling with the buttons to find the lights, it all went well. I think it went much better than the driving lesson, but then again: there I wasn't nervous about someone evaluating me.

When will I drive again? I don't know. Maybe this weekend, maybe not. It's good that I did it. I will try not to worry too much, and just do it often enough to not be as anxious about it again.
