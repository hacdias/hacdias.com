January started with a beautiful blanket of snow, which I've made sure to [share](/2026/01/03/wielewaal/) [multiple](/2026/01/04/snow/) times. I really enjoy snow, and the "opportunities" it offers. I've even made a snow angel! That was the first one of my life, but I hope not the last!

Fortunately, the snow cleared up before it was time to go [vote](/2026/01/12/portuguese-presidential-elections/) in the Portuguese presidential elections, because the trains in the Netherlands are snowflakes - you see what I did there? As expected, none of the 11 *valid* candidates had a majority, so a second round is coming next weekend. My desire to go and spend around five hours of my life is not that high, but I'm planning to do it anyways.

Other than that, no other major event happened this month. Some other things I will mention below. I've also visited friends in other cities, and also had a friend visiting us from the UK. And last weekend I also went to FOSDEM, but a post about that will come later!

## üí™ Health {#health}

As I mentioned some time along the month, I [started](/2026/01/16/swimming/) swimming lessons, and I absolutely love it. I'm now in level 3, which is "half-way" there. But that's just because I already had good basis, now it'll take a bit longer to move up the ladder. I really enjoy it, I just think the lessons are too short! I might try going one more time to the swimming pool in some weeks if I have time!

I've also had my [Invisalign intake](/2025/11/01/recently/), which was a can of worms and I'm not sure what to do about it. It turns out that I not only have an open bite - which I knew -, but I also have a cross bite on the right side of my jaw. That explains why I tend to bite my cheek on that side quite often.

However, they think they can only fix the open bite, but not the cross bite, and they say that's probably also the case for more traditional braces: I just don't have a large enough upper jaw and the bone is not thick enough to be able to move those molars. So I'm at this crossroad trying to evaluate whether it's worth the probably 2 years of treatment to close my bite, or not.
